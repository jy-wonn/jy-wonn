Question 1: Why is one of the symbols used for exclusive disjunction {\displaystyle \not \equiv }{\displaystyle \not \equiv }?

The output is true if either of the inputs are true but both are not true.

Section 2

Question 1: Why is the formula for determining the number of rows in a truth table related only to the number of inputs, and why is the formula {\displaystyle 2^{n}}{\displaystyle 2^{n}}?

The formula is because there are 2n possible combinations of truth values, true or false. It is only related to input because the output is determined by the inputs.
